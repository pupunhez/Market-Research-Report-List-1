<p><p>The geotechnical instrumentation market is divided into wired and wireless types, each serving distinct needs in monitoring subsurface conditions. Wired instrumentation relies on physical connections for data transmission, offering robust reliability and real-time monitoring capabilities, making it suitable for long-term projects. In contrast, wireless instrumentation provides flexibility and ease of installation, eliminating the need for extensive cabling. This enhances mobility and reduces labor costs, making it ideal for remote or temporary applications, while maintaining effective data collection and communication.</p></p>
