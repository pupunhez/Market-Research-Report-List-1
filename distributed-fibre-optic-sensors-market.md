<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Distributed Fibre Optic Sensors Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Rayleigh Scattering Based Distributed Sensor</li><li>Brillouin Scattering Based Sensor</li><li>Raman Scattering Based Sensor</li><li>Interferometric Distributed Optical-Fibre Sensor</li><li>Distributed Fibre Bragg Grating Sensor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The distributed fibre optic sensors market includes various types based on scattering mechanisms. Rayleigh scattering sensors measure temperature and strain by analyzing backscattered light. Brillouin scattering sensors detect changes in temperature and pressure through frequency shifts in reflected light. Raman scattering sensors provide temperature measurements through inelastic scattering. Interferometric distributed optical-fibre sensors leverage interference patterns to detect minute changes, while distributed fibre Bragg grating sensors use periodic refractive index variations to monitor temperature and strain across long distances. Each type offers unique advantages for various applications.</p></p>
