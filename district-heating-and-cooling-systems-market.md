<p><p>District Heating and Cooling Systems provide centralized temperature regulation for multiple buildings through a network of insulated pipes. District Heating involves the distribution of hot water or steam to supply heating, often sourced from renewable energy, waste heat, or cogeneration plants. Conversely, District Cooling delivers chilled water for air conditioning, typically generated from ice storage or absorption chillers. Both systems enhance energy efficiency, reduce emissions, and lower operating costs, catering to urban areas and large complexes by optimizing resource use.</p></p>
