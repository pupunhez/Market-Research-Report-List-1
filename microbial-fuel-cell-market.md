<p><ul><li>Mediator Microbial Fuel Cell</li><li>Mediator-free Microbial Fuel Cell</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The microbial fuel cell (MFC) market is primarily divided into two types: mediator and mediator-free MFCs. Mediator MFCs utilize external chemical compounds to enhance electron transfer between microbes and electrodes, improving electricity generation efficiency. In contrast, mediator-free MFCs rely on direct electron transfer from bacteria to electrodes, eliminating the need for additional chemicals. Both types hold significant potential for sustainable energy production and wastewater treatment, catering to various applications in eco-friendly technologies and renewable energy sectors.</p></p>
